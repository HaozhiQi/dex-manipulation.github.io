Learning Dexterous Manipulation:
Design, Perception, Data Collection, and Control

Tentative CoRL 2024 Workshop, November 9, 2024

Introduction

This workshop delves into the frontier of learning-based approaches for dexterous manipulation, one of the most formidable challenges in robotics. It offers a comprehensive overview of state-of-the-art research and developments in dexterous manipulator hardware design, planning and control techniques for dexterous manipulation, data collection for dexterous hands, and sensing technologies, with a particular emphasis on tactile sensors.

Building upon the success of our learning dexterous manipulation workshop at RSS 2023 and dexterous manipulation workshop at RSS 2024, this workshop is tailored for researchers, engineers, and students possessing a robust background or keen interest in machine learning, computer vision, robotics, and associated disciplines. It serves as a platform for fostering discussions among preeminent academic and industrial experts. Accepted papers will be showcased through poster sessions and contributed talks, equipping participants with exposure to the most recent breakthroughs and avenues for collaboration, thereby charting the course for forthcoming innovations in robotic learning.

      Call for Papers/Demos

      In this workshop, our goal is to bring together researchers from various fields of robotics, such as control, optimization, planning, perception, hardware, etc., who work on learning dexterous manipulation.

      • Leveraging imitation learning techniques to advance dexterous manipulation capabilities
      • Exploring reinforcement learning and sim2real approaches for precise control of dexterous hands
      • Developing efficient data collection systems to support dexterous manipulation research
      • Investigating planning strategies and algorithms for dexterous manipulation tasks
      • Integrating tactile sensing for enhanced manipulation and multi-modal learning
      • Applying learning methods to optimize robot hand design and sensor configurations
